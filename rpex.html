What is Robotics Principles - Explore (RPEX)?

Each session of the program will be 9 weeks long, 2 sessions each, and be priced at $180 per student. Students coming from low-income backgrounds can apply to have their fee discounted or waived, provided proof of low income. Each 1.5-hour session will contain a 0.5-1 hour lesson, followed by a 0.5-1 hour workshop time, where students can make their own robots. While our summer camp, RoboCamp, follows a competition based format to simulate the First Lego League competition, we intend for this curriculum to be more poised towards project-based learning.

The curriculum is intended to be a complete introduction to robotics through LEGO Spike Prime, so mentors may aid students frequently.

The program does introduce students to Python, a high-level text based programming language, but due to the simplicity of the languageâ€™s syntax, and the fact that the more advanced features will not be utilized, we believe that Python will be a suitable way to learn programming, as it is not only easy to learn, but an industry-desired skill.

Admissions

Student admission will be stratified per school.

Schools have been chosen and provided seats based on the following criteria:

  • The existence of Robotics or STEM programs, which alumni of this program can continue to practice their skills in
  • Geographic location relative to Whitney


Schools are provided with a number of students to send to this program, so they can then choose students who would be a good fit for the environment.
The program will gather attendance from as many schools as possible, disregarding the community and socioeconomic background that the students come from.

All applicants are expected to embody the traits and ideals of Whitney student candidates, as we intend for this program to give them a bit of insight to the Whitney campus life.
